What is it and how do I use it?

Terminology can be a major barrier to learning a new subject. Ideally students would have a glossary of terms before they start studying. This program helps to make that list by identifying the most important words of each chapter.

To use this tool you need to upload the PDF file to be analyzed and input either the chapter page numbers or a phrase that occurs at the beginningi of each chapter.

How does it work?

First the PDF is converted into text and then seperated into seperate chapters. It can find the chapter borders through either a list of numbers (seperated by spaces) for each chapter or a key phrase in the form of a 'Regular Expression' (WIP) that marks the start of a chapter e.g. "In this chapter..."

Once the chapter borders are finalized it removes any stop words and utilizes TF-IDF to collect the most important words for each chapter.

Why am I getting bad results?

PDF's might be nice to read but they can be a complete mess underneath the hood. Occasionally there are words within the document that control formatiing but don't appear on the page. If the PDF is just a scan of pages then it is impossible to process the text.

The easiest way to check if your PDF can be processsed is to see if you can highlight text. If you can highlight text then the document can be processed.
